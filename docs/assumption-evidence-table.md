# Assumption-Evidence Table

| No. | Team Assumption | Evidence Collected | Evidence Type | Strength | Decision |
|---:|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Students and lecturers struggle because the PDF timetable does not update when classes are cancelled, moved, or relocated. | Of 21 respondents, 7 directly described the timetable failing to update for cancellations, postponements, or sudden changes. Real incidents included a room changed to a different building, a class cancellation announced only verbally, an exam room change, and a room double-booking that forced a lecturer to relocate mid-session. | Survey | Strong | Keep |
| 2 | Students and lecturers currently rely on informal or manual channels (group chats, asking lecturers, memory, LINE) instead of an official, reliable source. | Reported workarounds included confirming in class group chats, asking classmates, contacting the lecturer directly, relying on memory, and a lecturer manually informing students via a LINE group chat. Staff also reported manually explaining the same room change to multiple students individually. | Survey | Strong | Keep |
| 3 | A real-time digital timetable would be valuable enough that students and lecturers would switch from the current PDF. | 16 of 21 respondents (76%) rated the potential improvement 7/10 or higher, with an average rating of 7.5/10. 12 of 21 selected "Definitely" when asked if they would switch to a free digital version with live updates, and only 3 said "Not sure." | Survey | Strong | Keep |
| 4 | Existing university digital systems (RSU Connect, intranet, registration website) already solve this problem well enough. | Multiple respondents reported the opposite: RSU Connect showed a previous semester's timetable instead of the current one, and "most of the time RSU Connect bot doesn't show the room." This suggests current digital alternatives are not yet trusted or reliable. | Survey | Moderate | Revise |
| 5 | All users experience this problem at a similarly high frequency and severity. | Frequency was inconsistent: 14 of 21 selected "A few times," 5 selected "Almost never," 1 selected "Monthly," and 1 selected "All the time." Two respondents reported no real problems with the current PDF system at all. | Survey | Moderate | Revise |
| 6 | Push notifications for schedule changes are the single most desired feature. | Push/instant notifications were the most repeated request (mentioned by at least 5 respondents), but other respondents asked for calendar sync, a room/course search function, an auto reminder, or better mobile readability, showing the most-wanted feature is not fully consistent across users. | Survey | Moderate | Revise |
| 7 | Staff and lecturers face the same core problem as students, just from a different angle. | One staff respondent described repeatedly explaining room changes individually due to no shared source of truth, and both lecturer respondents described problems caused by the PDF not updating (a double-booked room and a sudden change not reflected). However, only 3 of 21 respondents (2 lecturers, 1 staff) were non-students. | Survey | Weak/Moderate | Revise |
