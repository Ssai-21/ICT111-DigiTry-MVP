# Customer Discovery Summary

## Project Title
SmartTimetable – Digital Class Schedule and Room Finder

## Target Users Studied
University students (18 of 21 respondents), with a small number of lecturers (2) and one staff member also surveyed.

## Number of Responses / Interviews
21 survey responses collected.

## Main Repeated Problem
The current PDF timetable, along with existing university digital systems (RSU Connect, intranet, registration website), does not reliably update when a class is cancelled, a room is changed, or a room is double-booked. Several respondents also reported that RSU Connect specifically showed outdated semester data or failed to display correct room information, meaning the problem is not fully solved even by the digital tools the university already has.

## Main Current Workarounds
Respondents rely on informal, inconsistent channels to fill the gap: confirming in class group chats, asking classmates or lecturers directly, relying on memory, re-checking websites repeatedly, or physically going to the student center. On the staff and lecturer side, one lecturer described manually informing students via a LINE group chat, and one staff member described repeatedly explaining the same room change to multiple students individually due to no shared source of truth.

## Strongest Evidence Found
76% of respondents (16 of 21) rated the potential value of a real-time digital timetable at 7/10 or higher, with an average rating of 7.5/10 overall. 12 of 21 respondents said they would "Definitely" switch to a free digital version with live updates, and only 3 said "Not sure." Multiple concrete incidents were reported with real consequences, including arriving late, going to the wrong room, missing parts of lectures, and a lecturer losing teaching time relocating a class. Several respondents independently requested instant push notifications as the feature that would build trust in a digital alternative.

## Weak Evidence or Unclear Findings
Frequency of the problem varied significantly: 14 of 21 respondents selected "A few times" experiencing issues, but 5 selected "Almost never," and 2 respondents reported no real problems with the current PDF at all. The most-desired feature was also inconsistent — while push notifications were mentioned most often, other respondents prioritised calendar sync, a room/course search function, an auto reminder, or simply better mobile readability. The lecturer and staff sample remains small (3 of 21 respondents combined), which is not yet enough to confidently generalise their specific needs.

## What We Must Revise
The assumption that all users experience this problem at a similarly high frequency should be revised, since responses ranged from "Almost never" to "All the time." The assumption that one single feature (such as push notifications) is the clear top priority should also be revised, since the feature requests were split across notifications, search, calendar sync, and mobile usability. Additionally, the team should revise the assumption that existing university digital systems (RSU Connect, intranet) already solve this problem, since several respondents reported these systems showing outdated or incorrect data. The team should also continue gathering more lecturer and staff responses specifically, since the current non-student sample is small.

## Decision for Next Lab
Continue with the same problem direction

## Updated Problem Statement
University students, and to a lesser extent lecturers and staff, frequently experience real, negative consequences (arriving late, going to the wrong room, missing parts of lectures, losing teaching time) because the official PDF timetable and existing university digital systems (RSU Connect, intranet) do not reliably reflect last-minute changes such as class cancellations, room reassignments, or room double-bookings. While the large majority of respondents (76%) want a real-time, notification-based alternative and many distrust the university's current digital tools for this purpose, the problem's frequency and the single most-needed feature still vary across users and should be narrowed through further validation in Lab 04, with particular attention to collecting more lecturer and staff responses.
